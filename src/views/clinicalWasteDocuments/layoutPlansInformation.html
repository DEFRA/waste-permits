Layout plans

Draw your plan to a size and scale that’s easy to read and include these features:

  • site location – add and label local details like named roads, watercourses and buildings
  • site infrastructure – identify and label main plant items, and provide this additional information:
    • storage capacities
    • types of waste that can be stored
    • emission points to air, ground, water and sewers
  • scale – to help locate items easily
  • key – to help identify the infrastructure shown on the plan
  • colour code – to help highlight different areas of the site
  • installation boundary – of the facility
  • other boundaries – of any waste operations on the site

Process flow diagrams

You need to provide a process flow diagram for each:

  • plant in the layout plan
  • treatment activity you’re applying for

Diagrams need to be clear and easy to read, using labels, symbols and colours in a consistent way to represent the plant and its processes.

Always include a key to explain the symbols and colours used in the diagram.

Your diagrams must show:

  • inputs – to each treatment plant, including raw materials, wastes and energy
  • distinct stages – of the treatment processes
  • outputs – including emissions and residual wastes
  • flow direction – of the treatment process
  • other details – such as bypasses, control loops, recirculation lines and connections with other plants
  • operational values – such as minimum, normal and maximum flow, temperature and pressure, etc.
