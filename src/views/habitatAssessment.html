If you have an emission point that is within the screening distance of a natural habitat that is a Special Area of Conservation, Special Protection Area, site of special scientific interest (SSSI) or Ramsar wetlands site, you will need to pay us to do a habitat assessment. And you have to provide an air dispersion modelling report.

The screening distance for an MCP and/or SG burning:

  • any fuel, where there is an SSSI, is 2km
  • natural gas or low-sulphur diesel is 5km
  • biogas, landfill gas or other fuel is 10km

If you are not sure if there is a relevant habitat within the screening distance of your site, then you can order a free nature and heritage conservation screen (opens new tab) or check using the Defra MAGIC mapping website (opens a new tab).
