We are the Environment Agency and we run the apply for an environmental permit service. We are the data controller for this service. A data controller determines how and why personal data is processed.

Our personal information charter explains how we deal with your information and your rights.

The data we need

The personal data we collect includes:

  • names, dates of birth, addresses, emails and contact details
  • details of convictions or bankruptcy
  • questions, queries or feedback you leave, including your email address if you contact us
  • your Internet Protocol (IP) address, and details of which version of web browser you use

We are allowed to process your data because we have official authority as the environmental regulator. The legal basis for processing this data is to perform a task in the public interest that is set out in law.

If you do not give us this data we will not be able to process your waste permit application.

What we do with your data

We use this personal data to help decide if the applicant is suitable to get a permit.

We do not use your data to make an automated decision or for automated profiling.

We put all the information in your application on a public register. If you can prove that some information is commercially or industrially confidential, we will consider removing that information from the register.

We may also use the personal information you give us to contact you about our service.

How long we keep your data

We will keep the personal data while the permit is in use and for 6 years after you surrender the permit. If the permit is for a landfill site, we keep the data for 10 years after surrender.

Where your data is processed and stored

We transfer your data through Amazon Web Services servers in Ireland. We store the data on Microsoft Azure servers in Ireland.

We will not share your data with anyone else outside the Environment Agency without your consent.

The data will not be transferred outside the European Economic Area.

This notice was last updated on 28 May 2021.
